HUT

A "hut" is a small, simple, and typically modest dwelling, often made of natural materials such as wood, thatch, mud, or other locally available materials. Huts are found in various parts of the world and have been used by different cultures and societies throughout history. Here is some information about huts: Characteristics of Huts: Basic Shelter: Huts are basic and rudimentary forms of shelter, often designed to provide protection from the elements and offer a place to sleep or live. Materials: Huts are constructed using a variety of materials, depending on the region and available resources. Common materials include wood, bamboo, thatch, grass, leaves, and mud. Roof: Many huts have sloped roofs to facilitate rainwater runoff. Thatched roofs made from dried vegetation are common in many traditional huts. Single Room: Huts are typically single-room structures, serving as a combined living and sleeping area.
